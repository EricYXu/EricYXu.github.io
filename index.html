Eric Yang Xu

Hello! I am Eric Yang Xu, a Statistics and Computer Science student at Harvard College. I am particularly interested in computer vision, machine learning, and statistical modeling, and how their combined application can improve patient care. My research interests lie at the intersection of artificial intelligence (computer vision, game theory, decision theory) and natural intelligence (particularly cognitive neuroscience and olfaction).

Last summer, I conducted machine learning research at the University of Pennsylvania GRASP Lab with Professor Jianbo Shi, where I developed and trained computer vision models that emulate human perception and perform advanced image manipulation tasks. I implemented a multimodal pipeline to change a person’s pose in images while preserving their identity by improving human pose estimation and image segmentation models.

Previously, I also conducted research in neuroprotection and neurodegenerative diseases under Professor Kahlilia Morris-Blanco, where I investigated promising therapies for post-stroke brain damage. I assessed the mitochondrial activity of neurons treated with novel demethylation enzymes and examined vitamin C’s therapeutic effects on cells in anaerobic environments.
